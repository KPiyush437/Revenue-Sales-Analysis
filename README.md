# 🛒 SuperMart Growth Strategy — BizDesign Showdown

## 📌 Objective

To analyze SuperMart's historical sales data and deliver data-driven strategies that improve **profitability**, **customer experience**, and **operational efficiency** across its three branches.

---

## 🗂️ Dataset Attributes

The dataset covers 3 months of customer transactions from 3 supermarket branches across multiple cities. Key attributes include:

| Category | Attributes |
|---|---|
| **Sales Details** | Invoice ID, Date, Time, Branch, City |
| **Customer Demographics** | Customer Type (Member / Normal), Gender |
| **Product Information** | Product Line, Unit Price, Quantity Purchased |
| **Financial Metrics** | COGS, Tax, Total Price, Gross Margin %, Gross Income |
| **Payment Information** | Cash, Credit Card, E-wallet |
| **Customer Satisfaction** | Rating (Scale of 1–10) |

---

## 🔬 Methodology

1. **Data Exploration** — Broad overview of sales trends, distributions, and patterns across branches, product lines, and customer demographics.
2. **Comparison Analysis** — Cross-branch performance, member vs. non-member behavior, and month-wise revenue comparison.
3. **Segmentation** — Customers segmented by membership status, gender, and payment preference.
4. **Financial Analysis** — Unit price vs. quantity sold relationship, COGS vs. gross income, and profit contribution per branch.
5. **Visualization** — Charts and dashboards built to surface actionable insights clearly (sales trend lines, donut charts, bar charts, scatter plots).

---

## 📊 Key Findings

### 💰 Revenue & Sales Trends
- **Total Revenue:** ₹3,23,000 | **Gross Income:** ₹15,000
- January was the highest revenue month (₹1,16,000), followed by March (₹1,09,000) and February (₹97,000).
- Branch leadership rotated monthly: Branch C (Jan) → Branch B (Feb) → Branch A (Mar), suggesting variable demand patterns requiring branch-specific strategies.

### 🏪 Branch Performance
- **Branch C** is the most profitable overall (Gross Income: ₹5,265), consistently leading across most months.
- **Branch A** underperforms relative to others, signaling potential operational inefficiencies or location-specific challenges.

### 🛍️ Product Line Performance
| Product Line | Revenue |
|---|---|
| Food & Beverages | ₹56,117.85 |
| Sports & Travel | ₹55,122.83 |
| Electronic Accessories | ₹54,337.52 |
| Fashion Accessories | ₹54,305.90 |
| Home & Lifestyle | ₹53,861.91 |
| Health & Beauty | ₹49,193.74 |

- **Food & Beverages** is the top revenue driver; priority for inventory and promotions.
- **Health & Beauty** is the weakest — opportunity for targeted campaigns.

### 👥 Customer Demographics
- Gender split is nearly equal: Female 50.1% / Male 49.9% — supports gender-neutral marketing with room for personalization.
- Membership programs show strong adoption, indicating healthy customer loyalty.

### 💳 Payment Preferences
- Cash: **35%** | E-wallet: **34%** | Credit Card: **31%**
- Near-equal distribution with a slight cash preference; growing e-wallet adoption signals readiness for digital payment incentives.

---

## ✅ Recommendations

**Short-Term**
- Prioritize Food & Beverages with prominent shelf placement and targeted promotions.
- Launch e-wallet cashback incentives to accelerate digital payment adoption.
- Investigate Branch A's operational gaps and introduce seasonal discounts during February (lowest revenue month).

**Long-Term**
- Expand Branch C's model to new high-potential locations.
- Introduce tiered premium loyalty/membership programs.
- Implement AI-driven customer insights for personalized marketing.
- Partner with local vendors for exclusive in-store promotions.
- Expand high-performing product lines (Food & Beverages, Electronics Accessories) while assessing new category demand.
